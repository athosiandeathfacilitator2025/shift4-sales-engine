## Tab 1 — Industries

**When to use:** Before a call, to get the right opener and hook for that merchant type.

### How to use:
1. Tap the industry that matches your prospect (Hospitality, Retail, Health & Beauty, Trades & Services, Professional Services, Healthcare)
2. Read the **Opening Hook** — this is your first 10 seconds
3. Note the **Call Time** — expected call length for that industry
4. Check **Reconciliation Watch-out** — raise this as a pain point during the call
5. Use **Buyer Signals** to know when to go for the close
6. If they push back, check **Why They Reject** and use the **Pivot** suggestion

### Pro tips:
- Read the **Walk-Away Criteria** before the call — know when this deal isn't worth chasing
- The **Annual Revenue** range tells you how large the prize is and how hard to fight for it

## Tab 2 — Battle Cards

**When to use:** Prospect mentions a competitor ("we're with Square", "we looked at Tyro").

### How to use:
1. Find the competitor card (Square, Tyro, Zeller, Big 4 Banks, PayPal/Zettle)
2. Read **Their Strengths** — acknowledge these, never dismiss them
3. Lead with **Counter-Script** — exact words to say
4. Know **Win When** — these are the conditions where you should push hard
5. Know **Lose When** — these are signals to either reframe or walk away gracefully

### Pro tips:
- Never trash the competitor by name. Use "other providers" unless you're quoting a specific fact (like the Tyro outage)
- The **Counter-Script** is a starting point — adapt it to the conversation

## Tab 3 — Objections (Challenger Sale)

**When to use:** Any time you hit resistance. This is your most important tab during a live call.

### Understanding the Challenger Header Card
At the top of the Objections tab you'll see the **Challenger Mindset** card with three moves:

- **Teach** — Share an insight they haven't heard before. You're not selling, you're educating.
- **Tailor** — Connect that insight to *their* specific business pain.
- **Take Control** — Guide the conversation. Don't wait to be led.

The **Universal Challenge Questions** at the bottom of that card work for almost any objection — tap one to copy it to your clipboard.

### Finding the right objection card
- **Search bar** — type a keyword (e.g. "contract", "rates", "time")
- Each card shows a **stage badge** (when in the call this typically comes up)
- Each card shows a **technique badge** (the sales move to use)

### Inside an expanded objection card

**Tonality guide (top)** — 🎙️ How to deliver the response. Read this *before* you speak.
- Example: "Warm, empathetic. Match their energy before shifting it."
- This is as important as the words you say.

**What's really happening** — The real reason behind the objection. Helps you respond to the *true* concern, not the surface objection.

**Rebuttal** — Exact words to say. Delivered in quotes — say it close to verbatim.

**Do NOT say** — Common mistakes that kill the deal. Read this before every call.

**Challenger Reframe ⚔️** — A deeper teaching insight. Use this when the rebuttal alone doesn't shift them. It changes their frame of reference, not just their answer.

**Challenge Questions ⚡** — 4 questions that put them on the back foot (in the best way). These get them thinking, not defending. Tap any question to copy it to clipboard. A ✓ confirms it's copied.

**Re-engaging question** — Use this to restart momentum after a tough moment.

### Technique Guide

| Badge | What it means |
|---|---|
| Pattern Interrupt | Break their auto-pilot. Drop pace, pause, let it land. |
| Feel / Felt / Found | "I understand how you feel. Others felt the same. Here's what they found." |
| Micro-Commitment | Ask for seconds, not a decision. Lower the bar. |
| Gatekeeping Control | Don't accept the brush-off. Stay warm but stay in. |
| Collaborative Frame | Make them feel included. Get permission to continue. |
| Evidence Reframe | Let data do the work. Don't argue — show. |
| Long-Game Plant | No pressure sell today. Plant a seed for when their contract ends. |
| ROI Bridge | Don't talk price. Talk cost of doing nothing. |
| Authority Pivot | Be the most informed person in the room. |

## Tab 5 — BNA (Business Needs Analysis)

**When to use:** First qualifying call. Run through all 7 questions in order before pitching anything.

### Why this matters
You can't pitch the right solution if you don't know their setup. The BNA also surfaces pain they don't know they have — which sets up your Challenger reframe.

### The 7 questions

**Q1: Who's your current payment provider?**
- Green flag: Unhappy with service, heard of Shift4/Smartpay
- Red flag: "I really love [provider]" with no pain
- Listen for: Frustration, uncertainty about fees

**Q2: What does your monthly processing bill look like?**
- Green flag: "I have no idea" (they're not watching costs) OR a high number
- Red flag: Already on cost-plus at a very low rate
- Listen for: Blended rate confusion — this is an opening

**Q3: How many terminals do you have? What type?**
- Green flag: Outdated hardware, or too many for the venue size
- Red flag: Just upgraded
- Listen for: Hardware pain, reliability complaints

**Q4: What POS system are you using?**
- Green flag: One we integrate with
- Red flag: A proprietary closed system
- Listen for: Integration frustration, manual reconciliation

**Q5: What's your average transaction size?**
- Green flag: High-ticket (surcharging/flat rate saves them more)
- Red flag: Micro-transactions only
- Listen for: Mix of card types (credit vs debit)

**Q6: What's your annual card turnover?**
- Green flag: High volume = high savings potential
- Red flag: Very low volume, unlikely to care about fees
- Listen for: Growth plans that would increase volume

**Q7: How many days a week do you trade?**
- Green flag: 7 days, high-volume periods
- Red flag: Part-time or seasonal only
- Listen for: Weekend trading (busy periods = reliability matters)

### Using the BNA results
After Q7, you have everything you need for the AIDA close (Lost Leads tab → AIDA section). Plug their answers into the Desire and Action steps.

## Tab 6 — Lost Leads

**When to use:** Calling back someone who showed interest but went cold.

### Re-Engagement Scripts
Three scripts by lead age:
- **Under 3 months** — They remember you. Use the RBA ban as a new hook.
- **3–6 months** — They've half-forgotten. Reference their original pain + what's changed.
- **6+ months** — Treat it like a fresh call. New information only.

### Why They Fell Off
Before calling a cold lead, check this section to diagnose *why* they went cold — it tells you the angle to open with.

### AIDA Framework
Use this to structure your re-engagement call:
- **Attention** — Lead with something new (the RBA ban, a competitor's price hike, their industry news)
- **Interest** — Connect it to their specific pain (from your original BNA notes)
- **Desire** — Show them what's possible (savings calculation, next-day settlement, support)
- **Action** — Assumptive close: "Let's get your statement reviewed this week — Thursday or Friday work?"

### On-Brand Rules
These are the consistency principles for the Shift4 pitch style:
- Always lead with the merchant's problem, not Shift4's features
- Never use "cheap" — use "cost-effective" or "efficient"
- Always offer a specific next step — no open-ended "I'll follow up"
- Surcharging pitch is evolving — don't lead with it, use it as a support point

## Tab 7 — Coaching

**When to use:** Daily check-in. Track your performance, see where you're winning and losing.

### Run Rate Card (top)
Shows your live performance for today:

- **Daily Target** — tap the pencil icon to set your call goal for the day (saves between sessions)
- **Calls Today** — how many you've logged so far
- **Projected EOD** — where you'll land by 5pm at your current pace
- **FCR Bar** — your First-Call Close Rate with a 60% target marker
  - Green = at or above 60% FCR
  - Amber = 40–59%
  - Red = below 40%

### How FCR is calculated
FCR = wins on first-contact calls ÷ total first-contact calls × 100

A call counts as "first contact" when you tick "First ever contact with this prospect?" in the log modal.

### Intervention alerts
The app watches your log and fires coaching prompts automatically:
- **3+ "Not Interested" in a row** → opens the "not-interested" objection card
- **2+ "No Decision Maker" calls** → suggests running a BNA qualifying call next
- **FCR drops below 40%** → flags Challenger Sale review

### Call log table
Every call you've logged appears here. Columns:
- Time, Outcome, Type (Inbound/Outbound), First Contact, Stage, Objection, Scores, Weight

### Industry breakdown (bottom)
Shows FCR and average call weight broken down by industry — tells you where you're winning and where to focus.

## Live Listen Panel (bottom-left, floating)

**What it does:** Listens to another browser tab's audio (your softphone) and your mic — detects objection keywords in real time and suggests the right rebuttal card.

### How to start
1. Tap the **🎤 Live Listen** button (you may need to scroll down to find it, or look bottom-left)
2. Chrome shows a screen-share picker — select the tab with your softphone
3. The panel shows a **LIVE** badge and an audio level bar

### What you'll see during a call
- **Audio bar** — confirms the tab's audio is being captured (green = loud, amber = moderate, blue = quiet)
- **⚠️ Objection Detected** — fires automatically when the customer says something matching a known objection phrase (e.g. "happy with", "no budget", "send me an email")
- **🟢 Buying Signals** — fires when positive phrases are detected (e.g. "sounds interesting", "how does it work")
- **Transcript** — your mic is transcribed in real time (en-AU)

### Ping-Pong Mode 🏓
Tap the **🏓** button in the panel header to toggle Ping-Pong mode.

- **Left card (red):** CUSTOMER SAYS — shows the objection phrasing
- **Right card (blue):** YOU SAY — shows the rebuttal + technique + tonality hint
- Use **◀ / ▶** to manually flip through all 9 objections
- When an objection is detected automatically, Ping-Pong jumps to that objection instantly

### Grade Call button
At the end of a call, tap **Grade Call →** — it:
1. Stops the audio capture
2. Pre-fills the Log Call modal with the detected objection and the last 400 chars of transcript
3. You fill in the scores and outcome, then save

### Stop button
Ends the capture session without logging. Use if the call ended before you were ready to grade.

> Audio is never recorded or sent anywhere. Analysis happens entirely in your browser.

## Troubleshooting

**App won't open:** Make sure the terminal window (from LAUNCH.bat) is still open. Don't close it while using the app.

**Live Listen says "permission denied":** Chrome needs you to pick a tab. Click "Start Listening" → a Chrome dialog appears → select your softphone tab → click Share.

**Speech recognition not working:** Live Listen mic transcription only works in Chrome or Chrome-based browsers. Firefox is not supported.

**Call log disappeared:** If you cleared your browser cache and aren't using Supabase, the log is gone. Set up Supabase (see the deployment guide) to persist across sessions.

**Ping-Pong mode blank:** This means OBJECTIONS array data is missing or there's a JS error — open Chrome DevTools (F12) and check the Console tab.
